PROJECT

1. Silicon Protective Layer for Space Components

Description: Implemented silicon as a protective layer for space components to enhance durability and resilience. My Role: Leading the research and development efforts to implement silicon as a protective barrier in space technology. Key Achievements: Developed innovative techniques for enhancing the durability and resilience of space equipment.

2. Kapton Material Research for Satellite Protection

Description: Involved in researching the use of Kapton material to shield satellite components from extreme space conditions. My Role: Collaborating with a team to explore the effectiveness of Kapton in safeguarding satellite technology. Key Achievements: Contributed insights into the potential applications of Kapton for space mission durability.

3. Cost-Efficient Nano Satellite for Pollution Monitoring

Description: Developed a cost-effective nano satellite (Version 1) to measure pollutants in stratospheric regions. Purpose: Aimed to provide an accessible solution for monitoring pollutants, empowering communities to assess their environmental impact. Key Achievements: Designed and developed a functional nano satellite capable of pollutant measurement.

4. Enhanced Nano Satellite for Advanced Data Collection

Description: Building upon the success of Version 1, this project introduced an upgraded nano satellite for comprehensive environmental monitoring in the stratosphere. Key Achievements: Expanded the capabilities of nano satellite technology for enhanced data collection and analysis, enabling a deeper understanding of environmental dynamics. These projects highlight my commitment to innovation and problem-solving in the field of space technology.
